Competition Overview

The International Youth Robot Competition (IYRC) is a global robotics competition that brings together young robotics enthusiasts from around the world. The 2018 edition held in Bangkok, Thailand attracted over 2,000 participants from more than 30 countries.

As the technical lead of my team, I was responsible for the core technology development of our soccer robot, focusing on autonomous navigation, object recognition, and strategic gameplay algorithms.

The robot soccer category required teams to design and build autonomous robots capable of playing soccer against opponent robots. The challenge involved complex integration of hardware and software systems to create responsive and strategic robotic players.

Technical Challenges & Solutions

Vision System Optimization

Developed adaptive algorithms to handle varying lighting conditions in the competition arena, which significantly improved object detection accuracy.

Motion Control Precision

Implemented PID controllers for precise motor control, enabling accurate ball handling and strategic positioning during matches.

Real-time Decision Making

Created state-based AI algorithms that allowed the robot to make strategic decisions in real-time during fast-paced gameplay.

Hardware Integration

Designed a custom chassis and mechanical systems that balanced durability, weight, and functionality for optimal performance.

The most significant breakthrough was our implementation of a hybrid filtering approach combining VMD (Variational Mode Decomposition) and wavelet transform to process sensor data, which dramatically improved signal reliability in noisy competition environments.
